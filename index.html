Rules & Judging

How It Works

Eligibility & Conduct

  • Open to students from NBKRIST and all other colleges — any branch, any year
  • Teams of 2–4 members; all members must be present at check-in
  • All code and design must start after the official kick-off at 10:15 AM on Sep 26
  • Pre-built or previously submitted projects will lead to immediate disqualification
  • AI coding tools (Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) are permitted but must be disclosed at submission
  • Any technology stack is allowed — web, mobile, hardware, or hybrid
  • Final deliverable must be a working prototype, not just a slide deck
  • Respectful conduct towards all participants, mentors, and organizers is mandatory

Judging Criteria

Innovation & Creativity25%
Technical Complexity25%
Completeness & Functionality20%
Design & User Experience20%
Social / Practical Impact10%

Judging happens in two rounds — mid-point review and final on-stage presentation, both in person at NBKRIST.
